Mouna Raga

By Kaliyuga Raman (a.k.a Anamika) | Aug 14, 2025

It was a rainy day with chilly weather. The dark gang in the sky gave a break to their drumming music concert, while the sun trying hard to see its beloved Earth’s beauty. Darkness filled the silent and peaceful road, also the two eternal souls — thus becoming three by joining the sun.

In her life, it was just another rainy day until that moment.

It was a heavily raining, dark scene. She was waiting at the bus stop. Slowly, the rain gave way, allowing her to see a little further — familiar eyes leaning against the wall in the opposite bus stop, watching her. A flash from divine gave them a chance to see each other after years. Their eyes locked, and the strong heart leaned a little. The strong woman never felt such heavy in her heart for past few years. The moments been unshared, the undelivered letters, unspoken words floated in her world. Her mind stayed blank as if there were no past or future only in the moment. They remained silent for a while. Moments shared a millions with mouna…

The sound of a bike approaching her brought her from that world. She sat on the pillion seat. A few drops from her eyes fell near her three-knotted neck. The man riding the bike saw her eyes in the mirror, traced her sight, and glanced at the man standing opposite. A soft, gentle nod passed to him. Then he slowly and gently tapped her leg, as if comforting her.

She thought about the person with her — a soft, kind-hearted, understanding man with good qualities from the Puranas. Happy and peaceful memories with him slowly screened in her eyes. She remembered how her days felt light, how home felt comfortable; she had become a child again after adulthood. She remembered how the man made their life happy, just as he promised, giving her the years with no time to think about her pain. With comfort and confidence, she placed her right hand on his shoulder. A little peace bloomed in his heart after realizing she was comfortable to move. In time, a gentle and soft acknowledgment came from the eternal soul standing the other side.

The bike slowly disappeared from sight.Followed by the sun spreading its rays, lighting up the Earth and a life.

She remembered the words once said by the kind-hearted man long ago:
“You might be failed as Radha, but you never disappoint me as my Sita.”
He is truly a RAM.

The Story ended with mouna...
